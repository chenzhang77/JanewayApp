Stroller safety begins with choosing the proper stroller to fit you and your child’s lifestyle and needs.

  • Always supervise your child when he/she is in a stroller.
  • Make sure your child is seated properly and that the safety harness and lap belts are fastened correctly.
  • Use the brakes when taking your child out or putting them in the stroller.
  • Get a stroller that is made for your child’s age, height and weight. Newborns need to be able to lie almost flat in strollers as they cannot hold their heads up.
  • Always follow the manufacturer’s instructions for putting additional items or accessories on or in the stroller.
  • Never place purses on the handles of a stroller, the weight of the purse could tip the stroller backwards.
  • Fold and unfold the stroller away from children to avoid pinching fingers.
