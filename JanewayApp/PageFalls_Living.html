To minimize risk of Falls

  • It’s advisable to mount your TV if you have a flat screen, to the wall. If this is not possible, make sure that brackets and straps are used to keep it in place on a stand.
  • Make sure the TV is as far back on the stand as possible so that children cannot touch it.
  • Don’t have loose rugs on the floor which are easy for children to trip on.
  • Make sure all furniture that children can climb onto such as book shelves and TV stands; are sturdy and won’t fall over onto your child. “L” brackets can be used to attach furniture to walls.
  • Add protective padding around corners of coffee tables or any piece of furniture with sharp edges.
  • Install window guards or make sure that they won’t open more than 4 inches. Window screens don’t prevent falls.
  • Use socks with treads while in the house to help prevent slips and falls.
  • Choose lamps that have a sturdy base and cannot be knocked over easily.
