To minimize risk of sleep

  • The safest place for your baby to sleep is on his/her back, in a crib, cradle or bassinet. Health Canada suggests room sharing for the first six months.
  • Do not use sleep positioners or rolled up blankets to keep your baby on their back.
  • It is not safe for your baby to sleep for long periods of time in such products as a stroller, car seats, swings, bouncers or slings/baby carriers.
  • Overheating is a risk factor for sudden infant death syndrome (SIDS). If the room temperature is comfortable for you, it’s also comfortable for your baby.
  • The crib should be empty except for a mattress and fitted sheet.
  • Keep your home smoke free. Cigarette smoke is harmful and increases your child’s risk for SIDS.
