To minimize risk of choking

  • Whether a blind is up or down, make sure children can’t reach the cords. Install a cleat or tie-down device up high on the wall and use it to keep cords out of reach.
  • Do not put furniture that a child can climb up on and do not place a playpen near a window where a child can reach a cord. This may pose strangulation risks.
  • Make sure that button batteries in household products stay secure in these items.
  • Always supervise children when playing with toys that use button batteries such as musical greeting cards and children’s books.
  • Look around your floors for things that may have dropped such as coins, jewelry or buttons. Children can put these things in their mouths and choke.
  • Be aware of older children who may give a younger child food, toys, etc. that they could potentially choke on.
